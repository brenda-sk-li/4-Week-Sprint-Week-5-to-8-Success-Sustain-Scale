Weeks 5 – 7

The TACT Team Owns the Cycle

The facilitator steps back. No scheduled sessions, no hand-holding. The TACT team runs the inspect → surgery → refine cycle independently — building the organizational muscle to maintain the bot without external support. This is where adoption either becomes habit or fades away.

🔑
Facilitator Steps Back — TACT Team Steps Up
Weeks 5–7 are fully TACT team owned. The Super User runs the improvement cycle. Change Agents drive adoption. The Sponsor monitors and removes blockers. If the team needs the facilitator to run every refinement session, the bot was never truly adopted — it was borrowed.
🔍
Phase 1
Inspect
Run the Michelin Inspector mindset. Test with real work scenarios. Document what works and what doesn't — Like / Dislike, Input / Output, Why, How it should behave.
›
🔬
Phase 2
Diagnose
Read the testing log. Identify patterns across testers. One complaint is noise. Three testers flagging the same behavior is a symptom that needs surgery.
›
🔪
Phase 3
Surgery
Super User applies the Live Brain Surgery methodology independently. Targeted fix — one section at a time. Confirm before cutting. Validate after transplanting.
›
📈
Phase 4
Measure
Track adoption weekly. How many active users? How often? What are the feedback trends? Is the bot becoming part of daily workflow — or sitting unused?
🔄
Repeat as needed through Week 7 — then prepare for the re-inspection
🔄
🚀
Change Agents Lead Rollout
Change Agents move from testing to training. They are the internal champions who make adoption happen — demonstrating, coaching, and normalizing the bot within the broader team.
📊
Sponsor Monitors Progress
The Sponsor tracks adoption metrics and holds the team accountable. If laggards are identified — a plan exists to address them. Adoption doesn't happen by accident at this stage.
🧠
Super User Builds the Muscle
Every independent surgery the Super User runs builds capability. By Week 8, they are no longer dependent on the facilitator — they own the bot, the prompt, and the improvement process.
Week 8

The Michelin Re-Inspection

In Week 3, the inspector arrived for the first time. The kitchen wasn't perfect — that's expected. Weeks 4 through 7 were the kitchen refining itself. Week 8 is the return visit. The same fixed standard. The same inspector. Different results.

⭐⭐⭐
Did You Earn the Stars?
Week 8 answers four questions — and the answers determine what happens next. This is not a celebration meeting. It is an honest assessment of where the bot landed against the success criteria defined at the very beginning of the sprint.
S
🏆
First S
Success
  • ?Against the success criteria defined in Week 0 — how many did we hit?
  • ?How many did we miss — and why?
  • ?What do the numbers actually say — not what we hoped they would say?
  • ?Is this bot delivering measurable value, or is it being used out of obligation?
S
🌱
Second S
Sustain
  • ?Is the bot part of daily workflow — or still being used occasionally?
  • ?What is the adoption rate across the team?
  • ?Who are the laggards — and what is the plan to address them?
  • ?Does the Super User have full ownership to maintain and improve without support?
S
🚀
Third S
Scale
  • ?Did we discover use cases this bot can handle beyond the original scope?
  • ?Which other teams or regions would benefit from the same solution?
  • ?What is the rollout plan — and who owns it?
  • ?Does this success story become a template for the next sprint?
⭐
1 Star — Functional
Bot works under standard conditions. Adoption is partial. Success criteria partially met. More refinement needed before scaling.
⭐⭐
2 Stars — Adopted
Bot is part of daily workflow. Most success criteria met. Team is self-sufficient. Ready for controlled expansion to adjacent teams.
⭐⭐⭐
3 Stars — Exceptional
All success criteria met or exceeded. New use cases discovered. Super User fully independent. Success story documented and ready to replicate.
🎯
The Sprint Is Complete.
The Work Is Just Beginning.
A sprint that reaches Week 8 with a 3-star result is not the end of the story — it is the proof of concept that unlocks the next chapter. The methodology is repeatable. The TACT team has the muscle. The success story is documented.

The bot you built is the template. The next team inherits the playbook.
